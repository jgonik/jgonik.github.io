Julia Gonik

Hi! I'm Julia, and I'm an undergraduate at MIT studying computer science and math with a focus on machine learning.

I'm particularly interested in reinforcement learning (RL) and its applications for building autonomous and intelligent agents, robots, and machines. I've also studied NLP, computer vision, and deep generative networks with applications in robotics and cybersecurity.

Currently, I'm doing research with MIT CSAIL's Distributed Robotics Lab under professor Daniela Rus and postdoc Igor Gilitschenski. I'm working on adapting the OpenAI Gym car racing environment in order to use RL for cooperative multi-car team racing. The ultimate goal is to study the kinds of coordinated strategies that autonomous agents can learn independently. In addition to research, last semester I took classes in machine learning, algorithms, and Russian.

Outside of schoolwork, I'm the current head of logistics for HackMIT's Blueprint. I'm also the Vice President of Chapter Relations and Standards of Alpha Chi Omega at MIT.

In my free time, I enjoy weightlifting, swimming, hiking, reading, creating digital art, and bothering my cat Emerson.

Projects

Multi-Agent Cooperative Car Racing

September 2020 - Present

I'm working on adapating the OpenAI Gym car racing environment to support multi-agent team racing. The goal is to analyze the collaborative strategies that the agents learn.

Training Dreamer on Google Research Football

Fall 2020

As my final project for 6.867 (Graduate Machine Learning), my group and I trained the Dreamer RL algorithm on the Google Research football environment. Our agent achieved promising results with limited training time.

Playground

Summer 2020

As a developer for HackMIT, I contributed to Playground, a Club-Penguin-style virtual world used during our 2020 event. I mainly worked on Jukebox, which allowed users to synchronously listen to music on the platform.

Zero-Shot Imitation Learning

October 2019 - June 2020

During my sophomore year at MIT, I was an undergraduate researcher with Professor Pulkit Agrawal of CSAIL. I worked on training a small robot to autonomously navigate using independently collected image data of its environment.

GRAIL

Summer 2019

During my time at Raytheon BBN Technologies, I worked on GRAIL, which is a system for grayzone warfare planning. I designed the database structure and worked on anomaly detection for noisy real-world data.

Laser Harp Guitar Hero

Spring 2019

As my final project for 6.08 (Embedded Systems), my group and I designed and built a fully functional laser harp video game in the style of Guitar Hero. The game was written in C++ on an ESP32 microcontroller and interfaces with a Python-based server.

CyberGAN

Summer & Fall 2020

As an intern with NASA JPL, my collaborators and I designed a novel Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) in order to produce high-fidelity cybersecurity data. Our realistic data can be reliably used to augment existing datasets and test machine learning applications in cybersecurity.
